## Executive Summary

> Chow Tai Fook Jewellery Group Limited (SEHK: 1929) is selected as the subject company for this project. Founded in 1929 and listed on the Main Board of The Stock Exchange of Hong Kong in December 2011, Chow Tai Fook is the world's largest jewellery retailer by store count. The company is publicly traded on a recognized major exchange, operates in a non-financial sector, and has published audited annual reports for FY2024 (year ended 31 March 2024) and FY2025 (year ended 31 March 2025) — satisfying all eligibility criteria. The selection is driven by direct industry relevance: I serve as Head of Investor Relations and Chief of Staff at PNJ Group (HOSE: PNJ), Vietnam's largest listed jeweller, making Chow Tai Fook a strategically significant global peer in the same product category. The FY2024–FY2025 window captures a compelling analytical case — record revenue of HK$108,713 million in FY2024 followed by a 17.5% revenue contraction in FY2025, alongside a simultaneous 9.8% increase in operating profit — a divergence that ratio analysis is well-positioned to explain.

## Background

> Chow Tai Fook operates across Mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, and select international markets, with a product portfolio spanning gold, platinum, diamond, and gem-set jewellery under brands including CHOW TAI FOOK, HEARTS ON FIRE, and ENZO.
>
> The analytical period covers two fiscal years:
>
> - **FY2024 (year ended 31 March 2024):** Revenue +14.8% to HK$108,713 million; core operating profit (COP) +28.9% to HK$12,163 million; COP margin 11.2%; profit attributable to shareholders HK$6,499 million; EPS HK$0.65; dividend payout ratio ~84.6%; Mainland POS 7,403 (net +143 stores).
> - **FY2025 (year ended 31 March 2025):** Revenue −17.5% to HK$89,656 million; operating profit +9.8% to HK$14,746 million; operating margin +400bps to 16.4%; gross profit margin +550bps to 29.5%; profit attributable to shareholders HK$5,916 million; EPS HK$0.59; Mainland POS rationalized to 6,274 (net −1,129 stores).
>
> This two-year window captures a meaningful strategic inflection: from a record revenue year with aggressive network expansion to a deliberate contraction period characterized by margin resilience and store rationalization. The contrast provides strong analytical foundation across all six ratio categories in the course template.

## Method

> The analysis will apply the six ratio categories in `performance-ratios-template.xlsx` to CTF's audited IFRS financial statements for FY2024 and FY2025:
>
> 1. **Liquidity** — current ratio, quick ratio

## Findings

> *Preliminary observations based on published FY2024 and FY2025 annual results. Full ratio computation will be completed at Stage 3.*
>
> **Revenue and profit divergence:** Revenue fell 17.5% in FY2025 while operating profit grew 9.8% and operating margin expanded 400bps to 16.4% — a decoupling that DuPont and profitability ratios will quantify.
>
> **Gross margin expansion:** Gross profit margin expanded 550bps to 29.5% in FY2025, driven by higher gold prices improving retail-level margins and a shift toward higher-margin fixed-price jewellery.
>
> **Network rationalization impact:** A net reduction of 1,129 Mainland POS in FY2025 will materially affect asset turnover and inventory efficiency ratios relative to FY2024's expansion-driven asset base.
>
> **ROE above historical average despite profit decline:** ROE reached 21.9% in FY2025 — above the five-year historical average of 18.4% — despite profit falling 8.97%, suggesting leverage and equity base dynamics as key contributors.

## Implications

> *Hypotheses stated in falsifiable, directional form as required by the assignment:*
>
> **H1 — Gross Margin Expansion Driven by Gold Price, Not Operating Improvement:** I expect FY2025 gross margin expansion to reflect gold price tailwinds rather than operational efficiency gains, because management's own commentary attributes the 550bps improvement primarily to commodity price effects and mix shift — both of which are exogenous or cyclical rather than structurally earned.
>
> **H2 — Asset Turnover Decline from Balance Sheet Stickiness:** I expect asset turnover to have declined from FY2024 to FY2025 because the 1,129-store reduction compressed revenue 17.5% while IFRS 16 right-of-use assets and residual gold inventory on departing store balance sheets declined at a materially slower rate.
>
> **H3 — Leverage as Dominant ROE Driver in DuPont:** I expect the equity multiplier to be the primary contributor to ROE in both years, because an ~84.6% FY2024 dividend payout ratio structurally limits retained earnings accumulation, keeping equity suppressed and leverage elevated regardless of operating performance levels.

## Limitations & Next Steps

> **Limitations:**
>
> - **Two-year scope only:** FY2024–FY2025 does not provide a multi-year trend baseline; FY2023 data will be referenced contextually where available but is not a formal analytical period.
> - **Gold price exogeneity:** FY2025 margin improvement is partly driven by commodity price movements outside management control, limiting the degree to which gross margin ratios reflect operational quality.
> - **IFRS 16 impact on leverage ratios:** Lease capitalization inflates both total assets and reported debt, which will be disclosed when comparing leverage ratios across periods or against non-IFRS peers.
> - **Market data timing:** Valuation ratios will be computed at fiscal year-end share prices and may not reflect intra-year price movements.
