## Executive Summary

I am selecting Chow Tai Fook Jewellery Group Limited (SEHK: 1929) as the subject company for this project. Founded in 1929 and listed on the Main Board of The Stock Exchange of Hong Kong in December 2011, Chow Tai Fook is the world's largest jewellery retailer by store count, reporting under IFRS in HKD with a market capitalization of approximately HK$70 billion. It is a non-financial, publicly listed company with a minimum of two comparable years of audited financials — meeting all eligibility criteria. My selection is grounded in direct industry relevance: as Head of Investor Relations and Chief of Staff at PNJ Group (HOSE: PNJ), Vietnam's largest listed jeweller, I have a professional interest in analyzing a scaled global peer in the same product category. The ratio analysis will examine CTF's performance across FY2023 and FY2024, with particular focus on efficiency, profitability, and leverage dynamics in a franchise-heavy, gold-intensive retail model operating under different accounting standards than PNJ.

---

## Background

Chow Tai Fook operates across Mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, and selected international markets. As at 31 March 2024, the Group maintained 7,403 points of sale in the Mainland alone, following a net addition of 143 stores during FY2024. Its product portfolio spans gold, platinum, diamond, and gem-set jewellery across proprietary brands including CHOW TAI FOOK, HEARTS ON FIRE, and ENZO.

The analytical scope covers two comparable fiscal years: FY2023 (year ended 31 March 2023) and FY2024 (year ended 31 March 2024). This two-year window captures a meaningful business cycle — FY2023 reflects pandemic-impacted resilience, with revenue declining 4.3% to HK$94,684 million and core operating profit (COP) declining 5.6% to HK$9,439 million; FY2024 marks a strong recovery, with revenue growing 14.8% to HK$108,713 million and COP growing 28.9% to HK$12,163 million. Profit attributable to shareholders in FY2024 was HK$6,499 million (+20.7% YoY), with EPS of HK$0.65 and a full-year dividend payout ratio of approximately 84.6%.

The contrast between these two years — cost discipline under pressure versus growth with margin expansion — provides a strong analytical foundation for all six ratio categories in the course template.

## Method

The analysis will apply the six ratio categories in `performance-ratios-template.xlsx` to CTF's audited financial statements:

1. **Liquidity** — current ratio, quick ratio
2. **Efficiency** — inventory turnover, receivables turnover, asset turnover
3. **Leverage / Solvency** — debt-to-equity, interest coverage ratio, debt-to-assets
4. **Profitability** — gross margin, operating margin, net profit margin, ROA, ROE
5. **Market / Valuation** — P/E ratio, EV/EBITDA, dividend yield
6. **DuPont Decomposition** — disaggregation of ROE into net margin × asset turnover × equity multiplier

## Findings

*Full ratio computation will be completed at Stage 3 upon populating the financial model. The following preliminary observations are drawn directly from published annual results:*

**Revenue and profitability trajectory:** CTF demonstrated revenue recovery in FY2024 (HK$108,713M, +14.8%) following a pandemic-impacted FY2023 (HK$94,684M, −4.3%). COP margin improved 120 basis points to 11.2% in FY2024, suggesting disciplined cost management alongside revenue growth — a dynamic that profitability and DuPont ratios will quantify.

**Inventory and efficiency dynamics:** Management explicitly prioritized "reducing inventory levels and turnover days" as one of its five strategic priorities in FY2023, signaling that inventory turnover was under pressure. The FY2023→FY2024 shift — from contraction to 143 net new Mainland POS opened — creates testable efficiency implications.

**Capital structure and shareholder returns:** The FY2024 dividend payout ratio of approximately 84.6% (HK$0.55 per share) against EPS of HK$0.65 indicates an aggressive return policy relative to earnings, with direct implications for leverage and retained earnings trajectory on the balance sheet.

---

## Implications

*Hypotheses stated in falsifiable, directional form as required:*

- **H1 — Inventory Efficiency:** I expect inventory turnover to have declined from FY2023 to FY2024 because the net addition of 143 Mainland POS required proportionally higher gold and gem inventory stocking ahead of demand realization, increasing average inventory balances faster than revenue growth.

- **H2 — Profitability Compression Ahead:** I expect CTF's net profit margin to contract in FY2025 relative to FY2024 because a high fixed-cost store network makes margins vulnerable when revenue declines — consistent with the 20.4% revenue fall reported in 1HFY2025 interim results.

- **H3 — Leverage-Driven ROE:** I expect DuPont decomposition to show the equity multiplier (leverage) as the dominant driver of ROE in both FY2023 and FY2024 because a franchise-heavy model with gold inventory financing requirements structurally sustains elevated debt relative to equity, making financial leverage a more consistent ROE contributor than either asset turnover or net margin.

## Limitations & Next Steps

**Limitations:**

- **Fiscal year timing mismatch:** CTF's 31 March year-end differs from PNJ's 31 December year-end; cross-company ratio comparisons will note this explicitly.
- **Accounting standard differences:** IFRS vs. Vietnamese Accounting Standards (VAS) create non-comparable treatment of lease assets (IFRS 16), revenue recognition, and inventory valuation. Adjustments will be flagged where material.
- **Two-year scope:** Analysis covers FY2023–FY2024 only; multi-year trend extension is outside current scope.
- **Market data timing:** Valuation ratios will be computed at fiscal year-end share prices and may not reflect intra-year trading conditions.
