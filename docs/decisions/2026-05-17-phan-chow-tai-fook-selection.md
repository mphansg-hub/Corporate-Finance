## Executive Summary

I select **Chow Tai Fook Jewellery Group Limited (HKEX: 1929)** — the world's largest pure-play jewellery retailer — because its FY2025 results expose three teaching-grade tensions in one IFRS-audited balance sheet: gold-loan mark-to-market volatility, family control of ~72.48%, and a brand transformation that decoupled revenue (−17.5% to HK$89.7 bn) from operating profit (+9.8% to HK$14.7 bn). Two PwC-audited fiscal years (FY2024–FY2025) support directly comparable ratio computation across profitability, activity, leverage, and investor-return categories.

## 1. Company Overview

**Ticker / exchange:** HKEX Main Board, code 1929 (OTC ADR: CJEWY); listed December 2011.
**Industry:** Specialty jewellery retail — consumer discretionary.
**Business:** Operator of the Chow Tai Fook flagship plus HEARTS ON FIRE, ENZO, MONOLOGUE, SOINLOVE; 6,274 mainland-China POS and 87 POS in Hong Kong / Macau as of 31 March 2025.
**Market capitalisation:** ~HK$130 bn (May 2026).
**Reporting:** IFRS as adopted by Hong Kong; reporting currency HKD; fiscal year-end 31 March.

## 2. Selection Rationale

Three angles. **Capital-structure complexity:** gold-loan facilities (physical gold borrowed from bullion banks) are marked-to-market under IFRS 9; in FY2025 the non-cash revaluation loss compressed net profit by 9.0% even as operating profit grew 9.8% — an exemplary accounting-vs-economic-earnings divergence. **Governance concentration:** the Cheng family controls ~72.48% via Chow Tai Fook Capital Limited, well above the 25% "controlling shareholder" threshold under Vietnam's *Law on Securities No. 54/2019/QH14* (Art. 4) — a useful Asian family-firm comparator for minority-protection analysis. **Strategic inflection:** FY2025 is Year 1 of a pivot to fixed-price, IP-anchored collections (CTF Rouge and Palace Museum each ~HK$4 bn) with productivity-led network contraction from 7,403 to 6,274 POS.

Primary sources: HKEXnews (hkexnews.hk), Chow Tai Fook IR (ctfjewellerygroup.com), Bloomberg Terminal, S&P Capital IQ for the peer set. Reporting standard: IFRS; reporting currency: HKD.

## 4. Preliminary Observations

1. **I expect gross profit margin to remain above 27% in FY2025** because the mix shift toward fixed-price, IP-led collections (~HK$8 bn combined) structurally lowers commodity-margin compression, and gold-price appreciation delivers an inventory-realisation tailwind on legacy stock.

2. **I expect net margin to contract even as operating margin expands** because IFRS 9 mark-to-market on the gold-loan liability moves inversely to gold prices, producing a non-cash drag that decouples accounting profit from operating cash generation.

3. **I expect inventory days and the cash conversion cycle to lengthen** because Mainland China same-store sales fell 19.4% while the POS footprint stayed large (~6,300) and gold inventory carrying values rose with spot prices — slowing turnover at constant unit volume.

## 5. Ratio Categories Preview

Prioritised: **Profitability** (GM, OM, NM, ROE, ROA) — central to the margin-vs-earnings divergence; **Activity** (inventory days, asset turnover) — gold-inventory dynamics; **Leverage** (D/E, interest coverage, net gearing inclusive of gold loans); **Liquidity** (current, quick) — short-term gold-loan profile; **Investor** (P/E, yield, payout — 87.8% in FY2025) — capital-return policy under concentrated family ownership.

## 6. Data Collection Plan

Extract the consolidated I/S, B/S, and cash-flow statements from the HKEXnews-filed FY2024 and FY2025 annuals; cross-validate against the IR investor deck. Source LBMA gold PM Fix (USD/oz) and HKD/CNY/USD period-end rates from Bloomberg for commodity and FX sensitivity. Peer set: Luk Fook (HKEX: 0590), Chow Sang Sang (HKEX: 0116), Lao Feng Xiang (SSE: 600612).
