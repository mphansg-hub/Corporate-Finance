## Executive Summary

I am selecting Chow Tai Fook Jewellery Group Limited (SEHK: 1929) as the subject company for this project. Founded in 1929 and listed on the Main Board of The Stock Exchange of Hong Kong in December 2011, Chow Tai Fook is the world's largest jewellery retailer by store count, reporting under IFRS in HKD. It is a non-financial, publicly listed company with audited annual reports available for FY2024 and FY2025 — meeting all eligibility criteria. My selection is grounded in direct industry relevance: as Head of Investor Relations and Chief of Staff at PNJ Group (HOSE: PNJ), Vietnam's largest listed jeweller, analyzing a scaled global peer in the same product category generates cross-standard ratio insights directly applicable to my professional responsibilities. The two-year analytical window — FY2024 (record revenue of HK$108,713 million) against FY2025 (revenue contraction of 17.5% to HK$89,656 million alongside operating profit growth of 9.8%) — presents a compelling divergence between top-line and operating performance that ratio analysis is well-positioned to disaggregate.

## Background

Chow Tai Fook operates across Mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, and selected international markets. Its product portfolio spans gold, platinum, diamond, and gem-set jewellery across proprietary brands including CHOW TAI FOOK, HEARTS ON FIRE, and ENZO.

The analytical scope covers FY2024 (year ended 31 March 2024) and FY2025 (year ended 31 March 2025). These two years capture a significant strategic inflection point for the Group:

- **FY2024** was a record year: revenue grew 14.8% to HK$108,713 million, core operating profit (COP) grew 28.9% to HK$12,163 million with COP margin of 11.2%, profit attributable to shareholders reached HK$6,499 million (+20.7% YoY), EPS was HK$0.65, and the Group operated 7,403 Mainland POS (net addition of 143 stores).

- **FY2025** marked a sharp reversal in top-line performance alongside margin resilience: revenue declined 17.5% to HK$89,656 million amid macro-economic headwinds and elevated gold prices, yet operating profit grew 9.8% to HK$14,746 million, operating profit margin expanded 400 basis points to 16.4%, gross profit margin expanded 550 basis points to 29.5%, profit attributable to shareholders was HK$5,916 million (−8.97% YoY), EPS was HK$0.59, and the Mainland POS network was rationalized to 6,274 stores.

This revenue-profit divergence across FY2024→FY2025 — where a 17.5% revenue decline coexisted with a 9.8% operating profit increase — constitutes a strong analytical case for ratio analysis, particularly in profitability, efficiency, and DuPont decomposition.

## Method

The analysis will apply the six ratio categories in `performance-ratios-template.xlsx` to CTF's audited IFRS financial statements for FY2024 and FY2025:

1. **Liquidity** — current ratio, quick ratio
2. **Efficiency** — inventory turnover, receivables turnover, asset turnover

4. **Profitability** — gross margin, operating margin, net profit margin, ROA, ROE
5. **Market / Valuation** — P/E ratio, EV/EBITDA, dividend yield

## Findings

*Full ratio computation will be completed at Stage 3 upon populating the financial model. The following preliminary observations are drawn directly from published FY2024 and FY2025 annual results:*

**Revenue contraction with simultaneous operating profit expansion (FY2025):** Revenue fell 17.5% to HK$89,656 million while operating profit grew 9.8% to HK$14,746 million and operating margin expanded 400 basis points to 16.4%. This decoupling of revenue and profit — driven by gold price tailwinds on margin and strict cost discipline — is the central analytical story of the FY2024→FY2025 period.

**Gross margin step-up:** Gross profit margin expanded 550 basis points to 29.5% in FY2025, primarily attributable to the gold price surge improving like-for-like margins at retail level and portfolio mix improvement toward higher-margin products. This will be a key profitability ratio finding.

**Network rationalization affecting efficiency ratios:** The Mainland POS network contracted from 7,403 (FY2024) to 6,274 (FY2025), a reduction of 1,129 stores. This structural shift will materially affect asset turnover, inventory turnover, and overall capital efficiency ratios between the two periods.

**ROE improvement despite profit decline:** Return on equity reached 21.9% in FY2025, above the Group's five-year historical average of 18.4%, despite profit attributable to shareholders declining 8.97%. This signals leverage or equity base contraction as a contributing factor — to be quantified via DuPont decomposition.

## Implications

*Hypotheses stated in falsifiable, directional form as required:*

- **H1 — Gross Margin Expansion Driven by Gold Price, Not Volume:** I expect gross margin to have expanded materially from FY2024 to FY2025 — confirmed at 550 basis points to 29.5% — because elevated gold prices improved retail-level margins on existing inventory without requiring volume growth, a dynamic that should be visible in the gross profit ratio independently of revenue decline.

- **H2 — Asset Turnover Deterioration from Network Contraction:** I expect asset turnover to have declined from FY2024 to FY2025 because the 1,129-store reduction in Mainland POS, while improving per-store productivity, reduced the revenue base faster than the corresponding reduction in total assets — particularly given IFRS 16 right-of-use assets remaining on balance sheet during lease wind-down periods.

- **H3 — Leverage as the Primary ROE Driver:** I expect DuPont decomposition to show the equity multiplier (leverage) as the dominant contributor to ROE in both years because the franchise model, gold inventory financing, and an 84.6% FY2024 dividend payout ratio (structurally limiting retained earnings accumulation) sustain elevated debt-to-equity, making financial leverage a more consistent ROE driver than asset turnover or net margin.

## Limitations & Next Steps

**Limitations:**

- **Fiscal year timing mismatch:** CTF's 31 March year-end differs from PNJ Group's 31 December year-end; cross-company ratio comparisons will note this limitation explicitly and avoid direct period-to-period benchmarking without adjustment.
- **Accounting standard differences:** IFRS vs. Vietnamese Accounting Standards (VAS) create non-comparable treatment of lease assets (IFRS 16 vs. VAS 6), revenue recognition, and inventory valuation. Material differences will be flagged at Stage 3.
- **Gold price exogeneity:** FY2025 margin improvement is partly driven by gold price movements — an exogenous commodity factor — which limits the extent to which margin ratio improvement reflects operational performance improvement. This will be noted in the Findings narrative.
- **Two-year scope:** Analysis covers FY2024–FY2025 only; extending to FY2023 would provide a three-year trend baseline but is outside the current project scope.
